# Digital Taonga DAO Charter

## Purpose
- Define governance, membership, proposal, and voting rules for managing culturally sensitive digital assets (Taonga) and their access.

## Scope
- Covers proposals related to access, reuse, stewardship, and cultural protocols for Taonga datasets and derived works.

## Principles
- Community consent and leadership
- Transparency and auditability
- Respect for cultural protocols

## Review Cycle
- Charter review annually and after any significant governance or legal change.
- Amendment proposals may be triggered by community feedback, legislative change, or operational lessons learned.

## Membership
- Core members: nominated community representatives, Community Governance Lead, Curatorial Lead
- Voting members: community members as defined in the DAO membership registry
- Observers: legal, technical, or partner representatives (non-voting)

## Proposal Lifecycle
1. Draft: proposer submits a proposal with purpose, data description, intended use, duration, and mitigation measures.
2. Consultation: notify and consult relevant community representatives for feedback (timeline configurable per community protocols).
3. Posting: post the proposal to the DAO portal with metadata and voting timeline.
4. Voting: follow voting rules (quorum, thresholds) documented below.
5. Execution: if passed, execute access or stewardship actions and log outcomes.

## Voting Rules (example)
- Quorum: 30% of active voting members
- Approval: simple majority of votes cast, or supermajority for highly sensitive items (define thresholds per community choice)
- Voting window: typically 7–21 days depending on urgency and community preference

## Access & Consent
- Access granted only per approved proposal and subject to consent terms.
- Access audits required quarterly; revocation triggers re-evaluation and notification.

## Data Handling & Security
- Follow data minimization and least-privilege access.
- Use encrypted storage and auditable access logs for Taonga data.

## Key Risks & Controls
- Risk: consent misalignment or scope creep. Control: approve access only by explicit proposal and documented consent scope.
- Risk: inadequate audit trails. Control: maintain access logs with identity, purpose, and duration for all Taonga data access.
- Risk: unauthorized data reuse. Control: enforce approved-use restrictions and regular review of active access grants.

## Amendments
- Charter amendments require a proposal and a higher approval threshold (e.g., 2/3 majority).

Artifacts
- Membership registry, proposal archive, vote records, access logs, and audit reports.
